The Story Behind The Glory

The Night India
Reclaimed The World

It had been 28 long years since India last lifted the Cricket World Cup. The 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup final at the Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai was one of the most emotionally charged occasions in Indian sporting history — and a 22-year-old Virat Kohli was right at the heart of it.

I told myself — just give Sachin paaji a gift tonight. Get India off to a start and let the senior men finish the job.

— Virat Kohli, on batting in the 2011 WC Final

Batting at No. 3, Kohli walked in with India needing a steady foundation after the early dismissal of Virender Sehwag. He scored a composed 35 runs, providing the platform that MS Dhoni and Gautam Gambhir would use to seal one of the greatest victories in Indian cricket history.

After his dismissal, Kohli famously told the dressing room: "This one is for Sachin paaji." Those words captured the emotion of a generation of Indian cricketers who had grown up watching Tendulkar chase this dream. The six that Dhoni hit to seal the title sparked celebrations across 1.3 billion people.
