The Story of the Final Night

The Perfect
Send-Off

It had been 17 long years since India last lifted a T20 World Cup trophy. On the night of 29 June 2024 at the Kensington Oval in Barbados, everything came together — and at the centre of it all was Virat Kohli.

I told myself before going in — this is it. The team needs you. Just play the innings of your life, for all of them.

— Virat Kohli, on his mindset in the 2024 WC Final

Facing a South African side that had never lost a knockout game in T20 World Cup history, India needed someone to anchor the chase. Kohli, batting under immense pressure on a tricky surface, produced 76 runs off 59 balls — an innings that blended restraint, timing, and will in equal measure.

After India won by 7 runs in one of the most dramatic T20 finals ever played, Kohli announced his retirement from T20 International cricket — leaving the format on the ultimate high, with a World Cup winner's medal around his neck and the country celebrating.

Farewell to T20 Internationals

Goodbye
on Top

Minutes after India lifted the T20 World Cup 2024 trophy on the ground in Barbados, Virat Kohli announced his retirement from T20 International cricket. He had played his last T20I innings — 76 runs in a World Cup final — and walked away as a champion.

It was the perfect ending. No decline, no farewell series, no gradual fade — just a match-winning knock in the biggest game, and then the walk into T20I sunset. Rohit Sharma announced his retirement on the same night.
