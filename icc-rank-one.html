The Story Behind The Summit

Climbing to the
Top of the World

In 2013, Virat Kohli achieved a milestone that very few cricketers ever reach — the ICC No. 1 ODI Batting Ranking. It was the result of years of relentless consistency, hundreds under pressure, and the kind of statistical dominance that forces the cricketing world to sit up and take notice.

Reaching No. 1 means you've been the most consistent over a long period. That's what I always aimed for — not records, just consistency.

— Virat Kohli, on achieving ICC No.1 ranking

Between 2011 and 2013, Kohli assembled one of the most prolific run-scoring streaks in ODI history. He averaged over 50 in ODIs consistently, with centuries in pressure chases becoming his signature. Every high-profile series added to his ratings — against Australia, England, Pakistan, South Africa — Kohli delivered when it mattered most.

His ability to perform in successful chases was particularly reflected in the ICC rating system. While others scored in winning totals, Kohli was routinely winning matches single-handedly — and the numbers rewarded exactly that.
