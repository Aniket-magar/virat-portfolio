Career

2008
U19 Cricket World Cup 2008

Virat Kohli captained India to victory in the ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup 2008, showcasing strong leadership and batting consistency. His performance in the tournament marked the beginning of his rise as one of India’s future cricket stars.

2008
International Debut

Made his ODI debut against Sri Lanka on 18 August 2008 in Dambulla, aged just 19. Marked the beginning of a legendary international career that would redefine modern batting.

2011
World Cup Glory

Played a crucial role in India's 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup triumph. Scored the first run of the final chase against Sri Lanka, dedicating the victory to Sachin Tendulkar.

2013
ICC No. 1 Ranking

Rose to No. 1 in ICC ODI batting rankings for the first time. Established himself as the most consistent run-scorer in world cricket across all formats.

2014
Test Captaincy

Took over Test captaincy from MS Dhoni mid-series in Australia. Transformed India into a dominant Test side, winning series in Australia and England for the first time in decades.

2018
Fastest to 10K ODI Runs

Became the fastest batsman ever to reach 10,000 ODI runs in just 205 innings, surpassing Sachin Tendulkar's record of 259 innings.

2024
T20 World Cup Champion

Played a defining knock of 76 runs in the T20 World Cup final against South Africa in Barbados, helping India lift the trophy after 17 years.

2025
Champians trophy 2025

Virat Kohli was a key player in India’s ICC Champions Trophy 2025 win, scoring consistently and delivering crucial performances in important matches, making him one of the standout players of the tournament.

Family
The people behind the champion
PK
Father
Prem Kohli

Criminal lawyer from Delhi who enrolled Virat in West Delhi Cricket Academy at age 9. Drove him to practice daily, even in harsh Delhi winters. Passed away on 18 Dec 2006 from a stroke aged 54 — while Virat was mid-match in the Ranji Trophy. Virat chose to continue and bat the next day, calling it the turning point that changed him as a person.

Biggest support & inspiration
SK
Mother
Saroj Kohli

Homemaker and the family's emotional backbone. After Prem's passing, Saroj managed financial hardship while never discouraging Virat's cricket. In a heartfelt gesture in 2016, Virat wore a jersey with his mother's name on the back during an ODI against New Zealand.

Heart of the family
AS
Wife
Anushka Sharma

Celebrated Bollywood actress and co-founder of Clean Slate Filmz. Met Virat in 2013 during a shampoo commercial shoot. Married in a private ceremony in Florence, Italy on 11 December 2017. Known for her unwavering public support; Kohli has always defended her against online criticism.

Married · 11 Dec 2017 · Florence, Italy
VK
Daughter
Vamika Kohli

Born 11 January 2021 at Breach Candy Hospital, Mumbai. Name means "Goddess Durga" in Sanskrit. Virat left the Australia tour to be present for her birth. The couple deliberately keeps her face out of the public eye and social media.

Born Jan 2021 · Mumbai
AK
Son
Akaay Kohli

Born 15 February 2024 in London. Name means "formless" or "immortal" in Sanskrit. Second child of Virat and Anushka. Like Vamika, his parents are fiercely protective of his privacy and have not shared his photographs publicly.

Born Feb 2024 · London
VKO
Elder Brother
Vikas Kohli

Actor and businessman based in Gurgaon. Partners in Virat's business ventures including One8 Commune (restaurant chain) and One8 Select (retail). Married to Chetna Kohli; son Aarav. Known for vocal social media support of Virat, often clapping back at critics.

Partner · One8 Ventures
BD
Elder Sister
Bhawna Kohli Dhingra

Delhi-based homemaker who stays largely out of the spotlight. Graduated from Daulat Ram College, Delhi. Married to Sanjay Dhingra since 2002; children Mehak and Ayush. Active on Instagram (203K followers) and involved in the One8 Select fashion brand.

Involved · One8 Select

Cricket Mates
Teammates, rivals & bonds across formats
RO
Rohit Sharma
India · Batting partner
Long-time batting partner and close friend. Formed India's most dominant top-order duo. Rohit succeeded Kohli as Test captain in 2022. Shared three ICC trophies together — 2011 ODI WC, 2013 Champions Trophy, 2024 T20 WC.
Batting partner · 15+ yrs
ABD
AB de Villiers
South Africa · RCB teammate
Kohli's closest friend in world cricket. Together at Royal Challengers Bangalore since 2011, their partnership terrorised bowlers globally. Families are friends — wives and daughters share holidays. Kohli called AB's retirement from RCB one of the saddest days of his cricket life.
Closest friend · 14 yrs RCB
MSD
MS Dhoni
India · Mentor & captain
Captain, mentor, and friend. Dhoni guided Kohli's early international career; Kohli once said "MS will always be my captain." In the 2014 T20 WC semi-final, Dhoni famously stepped aside to let Kohli finish — a trust moment fans never forgot.
Captain · 8+ yrs together
KLR
KL Rahul
India · Close friend
Widely considered Kohli's best friend in current cricket. Their bond began at RCB where Rahul made his IPL debut. Often spotted together off-field, vacationing and training. Kohli's management agency also handles Rahul's career.
Best friend · on & off field
ST
Sachin Tendulkar
India · Childhood idol
Kohli's childhood hero and greatest inspiration. Played together in the 2011 ODI World Cup. In 2023, Kohli surpassed Sachin's record of 49 ODI centuries — a moment Tendulkar celebrated joyfully. Remains one of Kohli's most respected figures.
Idol turned teammate
RJ
Ravindra Jadeja
India · All-rounder
Vice-captain to Kohli in the 2008 U19 World Cup. Has played alongside Kohli in every major tournament since. Their camaraderie in the dressing room is evident; Kohli publicly praised Jadeja as world cricket's best all-rounder on multiple occasions.
U19 vice-captain → senior team
SD
Shikhar Dhawan
India · Delhi bond
Fellow Delhiite and close friend off the field. Their shared roots in Delhi cricket created a natural bond. Famous for mimicking each other and sharing light-hearted moments, Dhawan and Kohli were among the most entertaining dressing-room duos.
Delhi roots · close friends
JB
Jasprit Bumrah
India · Pace spearhead
Kohli and Bumrah formed the batting-bowling backbone of Team India's dominant era. Kohli's aggressive captaincy brought out Bumrah's best in Test cricket. Shared the 2024 T20 World Cup triumph together, with Bumrah named Player of the Tournament.
Batting-bowling axis
YS
Yuvraj Singh
India · 2011 WC hero
Kohli and Yuvraj shared the dressing room during India's golden 2011 ODI World Cup run. Yuvraj, Player of the Tournament, and Kohli struck a strong friendship. Kohli publicly paid tribute to Yuvi after his retirement, calling him irreplaceable.
2011 WC champions
RP
Rishabh Pant
India · Young gun
Kohli has publicly backed Pant on multiple occasions, calling him India's future and one of the most exciting talents in cricket. Kohli's management also handles Pant. After Pant's near-fatal accident in Dec 2022, Kohli was one of the first to send support.
Mentor figure
FDP
Faf du Plessis
South Africa · RCB captain
Kohli and Faf developed a strong bond through their RCB years and their shared competitive spirit as Test captains. Du Plessis captained RCB alongside Kohli and described their on-field partnership and off-field relationship as deeply respectful.
RCB partnership
SR
Suresh Raina
India · Middle-order mate
Kohli and Raina were teammates across all formats for India in their golden era together. Both are aggressive, high-energy cricketers who shared many match-winning partnerships. Raina remained a vocal admirer of Kohli well after his own retirement.
All-format teammate
