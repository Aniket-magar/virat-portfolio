The Story of Leadership

From Player
to Commander

When MS Dhoni stepped down mid-series in Australia in December 2014, a 26-year-old Virat Kohli was handed the Test captaincy in the most difficult circumstances imaginable — away from home, in the toughest conditions in the world.

I want to be the captain that never looks for an escape route. We'll play aggressive cricket, we'll look for results — always for wins, never for draws.

— Virat Kohli, on his captaincy philosophy

What followed was a transformation of Indian cricket. Kohli built a pace bowling unit that could demolish teams anywhere — at home or abroad. He made India fearless travellers, winning series in Australia and England for the first time in decades. His win percentage of 58.82% is the highest for any Indian captain with more than 10 Tests.

He resigned from the captaincy in January 2022 after a series defeat in South Africa, but his legacy as the most aggressive, result-oriented Test captain India has ever had is beyond debate.

Leadership Pillars

The Kohli
Captaincy Code

01
Attack, Always
Kohli refused to accept draws as a result. He set aggressive declarations, posted attacking fields, and pushed for results in every single Test — home or away, day one or day five.
02
Building the Pace Attack
Under Kohli, India developed the most fearsome pace unit in their history. Bumrah, Shami, Ishant, Siraj — he backed them unconditionally and gave them the pitch conditions to thrive.
03
Demanding Fitness Standards
No compromise on fitness. Kohli raised the benchmark for what it meant to be physically ready to play for India — and led from the front with his own legendary training ethic.
04
Never Back Down Abroad
India historically struggled overseas. Kohli changed the mental attitude entirely — winning series in Australia and England, proving India could compete and win anywhere in the world.
05
Lead by Example, Always
When the team needed a performance, Kohli delivered. His centuries as captain in crucial moments — Australia 2014, England 2018 — set the tone and expectation for every player.
In His Own Words

Captaincy
Philosophy

We don't play for draws. We play for wins. That's the mindset I've tried to bring to this team.

— Virat Kohli, on Test captaincy approach

I want the guys to feel that when they wear the Test whites for India, they're going out to battle. Full intensity, full commitment, from ball one.

— Kohli, on Test cricket culture

Fitness is non-negotiable. If you want to play for India under me, you have to be the fittest you can possibly be.

— Kohli, on fitness standards

Winning in Australia was something we'd dreamed about for decades. We believed we could do it, and we did.

— Kohli, after 2018-19 Australia series win
