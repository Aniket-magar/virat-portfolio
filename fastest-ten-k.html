The Story Behind the Record

Rewriting
History in 205

When Sachin Tendulkar reached 10,000 ODI runs in 259 innings in 1998, it felt like an unbreakable milestone — a number so tied to greatness that no one imagined it could be surpassed so quickly. Then came Virat Kohli.

Sachin was the benchmark for everything we dreamed about. To break his record — even one of them — is something I never dared to think about as a kid watching him bat.

— Virat Kohli, on breaking Sachin's 10K record

In 2018, in just his 205th ODI innings, Kohli reached the 10,000-run landmark — becoming the fastest batter in history to achieve the milestone. He was 54 innings faster than the God of Cricket, a margin so large it underscored just how otherworldly his consistency had been.

At the time of reaching 10,000 runs, Kohli had an ODI average above 58 — meaning he scored his 10,000 runs while getting out only around 170 times. Every record he chased, he broke. Every legend he respected, he surpassed.
