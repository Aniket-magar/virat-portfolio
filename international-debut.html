The Story Behind the Debut

A Star Is Born
On The World Stage

On 18 August 2008, a 19-year-old Virat Kohli walked out to bat in Dambulla, Sri Lanka, for his first international assignment. Selected for India's limited-overs tour, the young Delhi batsman was about to take his first step on a journey that would reshape the history of the game.

I was nervous, but I told myself — just play your natural game. That's all you can do on debut.

— Virat Kohli, on his ODI debut

India had recently won the ICC World Twenty20 in 2007, and the selectors were building a squad with an eye toward the 2011 World Cup. Kohli had been exceptional in domestic cricket and the U-19 circuit, earning his call-up on the back of consistent performances.

Though he scored only 12 runs in his debut innings, those watching knew they were seeing something special — a technique and temperament beyond his years. India won the match, and Kohli had officially arrived on the international stage.
